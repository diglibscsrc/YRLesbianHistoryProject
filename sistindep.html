SUPPORT INDEPENDENT BOOKSTORES

This photo shows the superstore's tactics in action. By opening shop directly across the street, chains can encroach on the existing clientel that an independent has built up in an area. When Borders moved in two years ago, sales began to decline, and to this day we are struggling to compete with the giant.

For twenty-two years, Sisterhood Bookstore has been taking a stand. Now we are asking you to stand with us.

Through all the years when womens's issues were "in" and through all the years when they weren't, we have been here for you. Although there are periodic media announcements that women have at last achieved equality, our oveflowing bookshelves, our crowded bulletin boards, and the posters on our walls tell a different story. We would like to be able to continue telling that story. This is why we are asking for your support. Our sales have been down due in part to the recession, the earthquake, and the proliferation of corporate owned chain bookstores.

Chain "superstores" have become a particular problem in recent years to all independent bookstores. While a chain store may carry some of the same titles we do, perhaps at a lower price, profit is always their bottom line, not the concepts and values in the books they are selling. The gender and lesbian studies section in a chain store will disappear the moment it ceases to be profitable. And that decision will be made thousands of miles away at corporate headquarters. Sisterhood Bookstore is a community resource that stands up for women's and gay rights whether or not it is popular to do so.

Our commitment to empowering women will never take a back seat to the profit motive.
