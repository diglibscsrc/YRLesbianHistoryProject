Date: Sun, 20 Dec 1998 17:40:47//

My name is Leona McElvene. I live in Detroit, Michigan and I am a friend of Ruth Ellis. This is just a note to thank you for creating and supporting a web page for Ruth Ellis. I was so excited and happy to see it. Sarah Uhle of Lansing, Michigan gave me the web address and said that you would love to hear from people who have met Ruth Ellis.

I first talked with Ruthie about 11 years ago. I was in line with other women waiting to dance with Ruth at a lesbian bar called the "Railroad Crossing". It seemed that Ruth did not tire of dancing. She danced with partner after partner. It blew my mind to learn that Ruth was in her eighties at that time. I was also told by one of Ruth's escorts, a white female German doctor named Morilla, that very few black women seemed willing to escort Ruth to places in the lesbian community. Morilla's comment both surprised and embarrassed me. I decided that I would become one of the black women willing to escort Ruth to places in the lesbian community. Ruth gave me her home phone number and told me that she loves to go places.

Over the years, Ruth and I have gone many places together. These places include: women's coffeehouses, dinners, parties, bars, lectures, conferences, church, women's music festivals, grocery stores, plays, movies, the symphony orchestra (she loves classical music), museums, and more. Over the years, Ruth has accompanied me through my romantic break-ups, job layoffs, job promotions, mothering my daughter, and more by being a source of inspiration and encouragement as I went through my changes.

Today, ... [we] talk on the phone about 2 or 3 times a week. ... I look forward to talking with my Ruthie on a regular basis and she is looking forward to her 100th birthday in July 1999. We are both hoping that she makes it to her 100th birthday and some.

Thank you again, Leona

Date: Sun, 24 Jan 1999 00:14:42 -0500 (EST)

Hello Ms. Ellis. I feel very fortunate to have found your site again. I happened upon it when my mother first got this Webtv in June, but could not find it again. I had forgotten to write down your name. All I could remember was that you had run your own printing business and that you were a Black Lesbian.

I am so impressed that there is actually a Black woman in the Life as old as you. Hats off and hail to you. I, myself, am 49 this past September. I have been in the life for over 30 years and am proud to be gay. I have never been in the closet because when I realized that I had feelings for women, at the age of 18 I just went for the gusto (lol).

I am a second year Carpenter's apprentice in the union. Whenever I get on a job site and the others learn of my age, they look to see if I can handle the work. I can and I do. I have only been on a bridge job and this past year, on an airport parking garage project (we built it from the ground up 5 levels).

Anyway, enough about me. I hope you don't mind if I tell your story and show the photos of you at our (SABIL, Sisters and Brothers In the Life) Black History meeting. There is also a women's group who will be honoring Black History (Herstory) Month and will be putting paintings and photos in the window of their building.

A couple of teenagers working on the project at the women's building asked me for some input about Black Lesbians, and here you are. How appropriate, I think, you being older and accomplished, and they being so young. I've given them some information on and pictures about Ma Rainey. I am also going to give them information about Tiffany St. Cloud, the youth activist that was inducted into the city of Chicago Commission on Human Relations Gay & Lesbian Hall of Fame.

...Take care and keep on trucking.

Gladys M. Brown
