DIRECTOR OF MAQUILADORA PROJECT AMERICAN FRIENDS SERVICE COMMITTEE (AFSC)

The Maquiladora Project is one component of the AFSC's Mexico-U.S. Border Program. The Border Program addresses issues such as immigrants' rights; causes and consequences of migration; international workers' rights in the context of economic globalization and the operation of transnational corporations; trade liberalization and economic integration; patterns of economic development and their impact on poor communities on both sides of the border; and Mexico-U.S. relations as they touch on these issues.
The Maquiladora Project focuses on the impact of the maquiladora industry on labor rights, health and safety, community environmental health, and the employment of young women as a vulnerable labor force. The Director of the Maquiladora Project will be based at the Texas-Mexico border.
