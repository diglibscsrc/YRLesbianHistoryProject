Quotes from our friends and customers

Customers at Sisterhood Bookstore

 

Lily Tomlin

 

"Sisterhood Bookstore means a tremendous amount to me. Many times, it has kept me going!! Seemingly an oasis, a sanctuary in a typical patriarchal US city. The hours and hours I have spent at Sisterhood could add up! The books of Sisterhood have opened worlds to me! I have grown and become a critically thinking feminist. (In the last 2 years I completed a MA in multi-cultural psychology.) I especially appreciate the Wicca Goddess Spirituality books which are finally becoming written and available. I hope to write a book soon and have it in Sisterhood!!" --Lisa Kohane, Los Angeles

 

"Like finding an oasis in the desert. I was recently 'out' in L.A. and basically pretty lost. I had never been in a bookstore like it before (this was 1982) and I haven't found another yet with it's ambiance and/or selection of books. I was so impressed with the atmosphere, I knew it was a place I wanted to be a part of, as it's been a part of me."--Rose Glen, Los Angeles

 

Alice Walker and Adele Wallace

 

"You anchored our exciting new movement--to a reader like me, the existence of a bookstore for and about us made our revolution real, visible, permanent. In many ways, you made a home for us--and you have never stopped. The parking problem and now the big chain bookstore have created terrible difficulties for you and us, but thank goodness you're hanging in there. You matter."--Harriet Perl, Los Angeles

 

"In the 16 years since moving to L.A. from back East, Sisterhood has been an anchor for me in a very personal way. The sweet scent of incense and ink, the sight of all those books celebrating women, even the creak of the wood floor under the carpet signal safety and nurturing of my lesbian soul. I shall not forget my first entry into Sisterhood while struggling to come out to myself and the world. The 'vibe' there is so positive, it always feels like the pressure of society is off me as I browse through your wonderful selection of books, music, videos and community notices. I am a frequent visitor. Thank you for being there."--Leslie Rabbitt, Los Angeles

 

Book signing with Alice Walker

 

"I especially treasure Sisterhood for the availability of lesbian periodicals, which are so essential to lesbian literature, thought and culture. With print runs of only a couple thousand, and all volunteer groups editing and publishing them, many journals, newsletters, zines, etc., would never reach their audience without Sisterhood. Also, providing a venue for authors to read to and interact with readers is invigorating to the creative process."--Tracy Moore, Los Angeles

 

 

 

"In this age of national chains dominating the retail book business, it is indeed a cause for celebration that this small women-centered bookstore not only has survived for twenty-five years, but that it continues in 1997, when it seems there's a Barnes and Noble on every other corner, to offer an alternative space to diverse communities of readers whose interests are not always represented in the context of corporate scale booksellers. I salute Sisterhood Bookstore. I commend its patrons for keeping this wonderful enterprise alive for a quarter of a century."--Angela Davis

 

"I recently moved to L.A. Sisterhood provided a warm, friendly and intelligent resource for the local women's community. It is far more than a bookstore. Thanks!"-- Anonymous response, West Los Angeles

 

"I've never actually been to the store, but from the very first time I ordered books online, everyone was incredibly friendly and helpful. I was so glad to find a bookstore that carried children's books that would actually encourage my daughter to read. I was beginning to think I was the only person on earth that wanted to raise a strong, self-confident daughter. Everyone at your store has been very accommodating and went way out of their way to get me the books I wanted. Thank you so much for all of your help!"--Michelle Faulkenberry, Santee, California

 

"Sisterhood Bookstore means women, communication, great expectations, high energy, information and future knowledge."--Jessie M. Ivy, Indianapolis

 

Simone Wallace and Gloria Steinem

 

"I came to Los Angeles 10 years ago, alone, not knowing anyone, but wanting to connect to the lesbian community. My only hope was the Gayellow Pages a friend had packed in my suitcase. Flipping it open to L.A., the Sisterhood Bookstore entry jumped out at me. As fast as I could, I drove down to Westwood and joyously spent my first real afternoon in L.A. soaking in the wonderful feminist woman-centered atmosphere. Way in the back of the store, I found the LN and the rest is history. I am so glad now I can give back to the place that gave me so much."--Thea Iberall, Long Beach

 

"SISTERHOOD BOOKSTORE is one of the great jewels of Westwood: a civilized, welcoming, well-stocked, and, above all, an absolutely necessary place for the many of us who struggle for gender equality. Congratulations to SISTERHOOD for 25 years of fulfilling a vital mission. I am sure it will have many more years of success. And we must all make sure of that."--Joan Palevsky, West Los Angeles

 

"One day, while wandering through the neighborhood, I stopped in and began to browse. I found a book, quite by accident, which turned out to be one of the most thought provoking works I ever read. It changed the way I think about things. Thank you. I think you have lots of books like that. So, every now and then, I come by and shop through your shelves of other peoples minds."--Tom Cook, Los Angeles

 

Simone, Adele and Holly Near

 

"Sisterhood Bookstore has meant that if I needed a source to open my mind, I could find it at your bookstore, if I needed a place to open my eyes, I could come to you. If I wanted to empower myself, I only needed to look to you for guidance, support and understanding. It is because of you that I came to call myself, proudly, feminist and womyn."--Suzan Najarian-Kojoian, Los Angeles

 

"Now that I am writing full-time, I can truly appreciate the wealth of information and wisdom from the many Sisterhood books which I feel make it possible for me to write. When my first book, Women Behind the Camera, was published this year, Sisterhood was the first bookstore I turned to. Whatever I can do to help women writers and the community of women readers, please count me in."--Alexis Krasilovsky, Los Angeles

"I have been coming to Sisterhood for 25 years. That same year I opened Of Books and Such, L.A.'s first children's book shop. We lasted 13 years. So warmest congrats!! In the last 5 years, I have been a 'student' of The Goddess and almost every book in my library has come from Sisterhood on this wonderful subject. And I thank you for that. I might add, most of my favorite earrings, necklaces, rings, candle holders, etc., came from behind your glass! Did I mention calenders and holiday cards? Long live independent bookstores, long live feminist bookstores. Long live Sisterhood!! Also, your workers are terrific!!" --Lindy Michaels, Santa Monica
