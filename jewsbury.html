LETTER:

Date: Fri, 30 Oct 1998 10:49:54 -0500
From: Tom Jewsbury
Tom_Jewsbury@inhofe.senate.gov
To: retter@skat.usc.edu
Subject: Geraldine Enser Jewsbury

I recently was "surfing the web" and came across your list of famous lesbians. I would like for you to please provide the documentation of this or remove our family name from the list. Who cares if someone famous was gay or not. The work produced is all that matters in the long run right? The self gratifying nature of your list is offensive to me and to my entire family. If the there is evidence of a gay relationship had by my Ms. Jewsbury, I assure you that she would have preferred it to have been private. I await your response.

RESPONSE:

Mr. Tom Jewsbury:

As noted in the preface to the notables lesbian list, we only list women whose names have appeared in reputable print sources or on reputable web lists. Reputable is of course, in the eyes of the beholder. Letters of concern such as yours motivate me to delve more deeply into the information concerning a specific woman and her reputed or acknowledged lesbian or lesbian-like history.

I am happy to provide basic references for you. More detailed information is done on a for fee basis.

See the following sources for specific information on her "intimate" relationship with Jane Carlyle and specifically Geraldine's own letters to Jane.

1.The Gay and Lesbian Literary Heritage, Henry Holt publisher, p 242-243.

2. Lesbian Lists, Alyson Publications, p. 23

3. Frontiers Journal v.4, #3, 1979 (M. Cruikshank) p 60-64 "Geraldine Jewsbury and Jane Carlyle."

I will suggest to you that since heterosexuality requires no proof of physical intimacy, the same standard can apply to lesbian and lesbian-like women. One reason for creating a list of notables is that for too long those who are threatened by female independence and love (of all types) between women, have censored and denied such information. Secrecy was the norm due to social opprobrium. In these relatively more enlightened times, we encourage the presentation of the whole person.

P.S.

Your letter raised several questions among those who saw it:

1. You say you were surfing the net and found Geraldine's name. Her name will not readily appear on a list of results if you type in Jewsbury or Geraldine Jewsbury on Yahoo and other search engines. This led someone to wonder whether you have a specific interest in lesbian history and/or you had some information about Geraldine's history, which led you to our site.

2. Another person noted your e-mail address as being senate.gov. Re your query, they wondered whether you were conducting official government business or personal business on an e-mail account paid for by tax-payers. [Jewsbury's e-mail address is linked to the office of Tom Inhofe, Republican Senator from Oklahoma].
