I don't know who did your research on Deborah Sampson, Revolutionary War soldier, but she was neither a woman of color nor a lesbian. As a relative, I can attest to both. There are many strong and contributing women who have also been Lesbians. It demeans and devalues your project to include people without proper research. Deborah was a brave and noble woman who believed that the battle against tyranny should not be limited by gender nor age. To this extent, she was a "feminist", however her reproductive and social roles following the war show her to not have been a lesbian. Additionally, not all people called Sampson are "of color". Deborah's heritage is traceable to Governor William Bradford, who came on the Mayflower, and to Edmund Hobart, an early religious figure in Hingham, MA. I would hope that your future research is better formulated than your past. Dick Bucknum.
