Record 26
Name: Vicki Randle
Website: Vicki Randle Page
Referred by: Word of Mouth
From: Venice, CA
Time: 1998-01-30 10:47:13
Comments: This is long-winded, which should suprise no one who knows me, but i just started writing about Johnnie and this is what happened. When i first met Johnnie, i was just twenty, if that. She had to remind me of the time and place when i saw her again, years later-the Los Angeles Women's Building, at a benefit concert i had agreed to do for a fledgling organization that Johnnie was trying to get of f the ground, the Alcoholism Center for Women. I thought it was really a good cause and i felt bad for all those women with drug and drinking problems that i had seen over the years, through the considerable fog of my own alcoholism, yet to become distinct and unambiguous to me. Many years of drinking, finally sobriety and a chance contact with the musician friend i had done this benefit with resulted in my being asked to participate in another concert, for the same organization, some twenty years later. It was through this symphony of irony that i was reintroduced to Johnnie. Johnnie was the kind of person one could trust, at once and completely. Her sense of ethics and justice and her kindness and love were so obvious and reassuring that even deeply suspicious people like myself found no obstacle to putting complete trust in her experience and opinions. Not that i didn't disagree with her opinions, of which she seemed to have an endless supply. Our first disagreement happened in our first conversation-how could it not? A conversation about the politics of the State of California between two incredibly opinionated woman, one an exemplary WW II veteran and the other, a Vietnam Era anti-war protester could not have gone otherwise. But she taught me how to disagree with someone without losing respect for them or dismissing them. I'am not gonna tell you how to live your life, but...I was how she'd address my concerns, giving me the inch or so of room that i needed to hear what she said without feeling that she was being domineering. in this way, Johnnie taught me how to perceive all the shades of gray between the extremes of my own dogmatic convictions. Johnnie also guided me through the fairly treacherous terrain of turning 40 years old. Her being 30 years my senior helped, but what helped more was that her life and experiences and decisions seemed to fly in the face of conventional attitudes about women and aging and what one is supposed to do at what age. I told her that i wanted to be her when i grew up and she laughed. I still feel that way and hope that i can grow into half the incredible human being that she was. Thank you, Johnnie. Grace and Rachelle: my heart is with you
