Peggy Case

Hands as soft as silk, weave welcome twining between our rough fingers. She wears her gifts wrapped inside tiny warm arms, embracing us in love.

She hold us when we walk, lets us believe we support bones too frail to step alone. But she is the anchor in each step. She holds the sky in place, guiding us to the dance.

Witness to a century, survivor of its crude hatreds, recorder of our memories, she gathers each moment into a bouquet of flowers, pressing them into her soul.

She wonders why fame comes, what deserving lies in a woman whose life seems so simple. She has climbed no mountains, created no monuments, cast few ripples on the pond of life.

But she is the monument we need most of all. When she enters our spaces, all that is good in us rises to meet her loving embrace. She heals our crippled hearts.

We remember who we can be, in a simple twining of fingers, bouquets of loving souls, alive to dance and ripple every pond.

Remembering Ruth
Nancy Melke

It is difficult to imagine the lesbian community here in metro Detroit without Ruth Ellis. For me, she has always been a part of its fabric. I was coming out at about the same time Ruth (then in her seventies) connected with the local community. One of the first meetings I attended included Ruth sharing her own coming out story.

Though I did not know her well, over the years we would run into one another -- at lesbian events, on the downtown streets near her home. And I would hear of her through friends, the local gay/lesbian media.

Ruth was always warm, funny, friendly, energetic. She was a link to a piece of lesbian history that I found nowhere else, and she shared it generously with us all.

After 101 years, Ruth Ellis was ready to move on; and I wish her a journey as remarkable as her life.

I feel grateful to have known her just a little, grateful for the history she shared. And I'll miss her.

Ruth and GOLDEN THREADS

Our beloved Ruth DID attend the 14th Annual GOLDEN THREADS Celebration this past June 22-25, 2000 in Provincetown , lovingly referred to as "P'town", and she danced again with so many of us. One of the most beautiful scenes was when she was dancing with America's famous drummer, Ubaka Hill. It was truly touching. But that was our, Ruthie...loving, giving and truly touching. These characteristics among so many others are what made her our icon.

It was blessed that Ruth, in passing, passed away peacefully in her sleep. I can imagine now that one of her many greatest fans, Christine Burton, the founder of GOLDEN THREADS is smiling with Ruth right now!

We all have our "most" beloved memories of being with Ruth and for the rest of my life I know that mine is monumentally the most beloved for me, because Ruth was a most important part of my marriage (my commitment ceremony) at the Provincetown Inn on June 25th, 2000 at 11 A.M. when my darling Judy became my wife. Ruth gave us the special "rose" for our life's long-lasting commitment and she blessed us in this unification of two becoming one. I learned then how much she loved us and on my way to Japan in August at my stop-over at the Detroit airport when I phoned Ruth, she first asked of my wife Judy and then lovingly referred to our wedding again.

Our love will always be with Ruth as it was while she was on this earth. Her presence has given us all strength in her beauty.

In Sisterhood,

Joy D. Griffith
