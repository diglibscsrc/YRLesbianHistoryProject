  • Feminisms

    • LJDigital: Feminism: Online Resources
    Library Journal is a source of book reviews for library selection, and LJDigital is the journal's WWW site. Paula Hammett has compiled links to a variety of informative sites pertaining to feminism (some of which have been duplicated here at Sisterhood's links page). (Added March 22, 1998.)
    femina: Web Search for Women
    "FeMiNa was created in September of 1995 and debuted online to provide women with a comprehensive, searchable directory of links to female friendly sites and information on the World Wide Web." (Added December 7, 1997.)
    SeaFATtle Home Page
    "We are a women's collective of fat feminists and their allies, dedicated to activism and support.." Here are inspiring testimony and polemics, tips for organizing events to celebrate International No-Diet Days and lots of links to related sites. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    The National Organization for Women
    Join NOW, read about news and enduring issues. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)

  • LesBiGay Resources

    • "This Way Out" Home Page
    " This Way Out is the award-winning internationally distributed gay and lesbian radio program, currently airing on over 80 stations in eight countries. The weekly half-hour 'magazine' program is produced in Los Angeles and distributed via satellite to stations around North America." You can hear This Way Out Tuesday afternoons at 3:00 on Pacifica station KPFK, 90.7 FM, and during the Sunday broadcast of the station's own IMRU. (Added December 7, 1997.)
    GLAAD Online
    The Gay & Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation has created a monumental site featuring GLAADAlert, the Alliance's "weekly activation tool." You'll find news reports, discussion of current and past media events, and exposure of anti-Gay/Lesbian bias in the media. (Added October 12, 1997.)
    PlanetOut: Land On It
    One stop shopping for the out set! (But there's more than just shopping...) (Added October 12, 1997.)
    UCLA Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender Resources Office
    Includes a list of LGBT groups at UCLA and a calendar of events. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    The Lesbian News
    The online version of the monthly news and events magazine. The site invites communication with LN. You can also subscribe and order back issues. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    Olivia Travel
    The travel agency that specializes in cruises and resort vacations for women. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    BiNet L.A.
    "BiNet L.A. is an educational, social and support group for bisexuals and bi-friendly people." (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    bi.org
    bi.org's motto is "Serving the world bisexual community." In addition to news and links to other bisexuality group sites, bi.org offers Web hosting for bisexual customers and support groups. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    The PFLAG Home Page
    Parents, Families and Friends of Lesbians and Gays. See also the home page for the PFLAG mailing lists, PFLAG-Talk and TGS-PFLAG, which are managed independently from National PFLAG. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)

  • Medical Matters
    Peace After Abortion
    "If you found your way to this page, chances are you're a woman who is hurting in some way after an abortion. This Web site is meant especially for you." The site features information about feelings caused by abortion, factors leading to pregnancy, personal life stories, support for men, and an opportunity to order Ava Torre-Bueno's book, Peace After Abortion. (Added November 23, 1997.)
    Komen.Org
    "Our Mission: To eradicate breast cancer as a life-threatening disease by advancing research, education, screening and treatment." Nancy Brinker, Founding Chairman, discusses her sister, Susan G. Komen, whose battle with breast cancer inspired Ms. Brinker to create the organization and its foundation. The site features opportunities for grants and funding for breast cancer research, and support and testimonials. (Added October 12, 1997.)

  • Women, Girls and Technology

    • Women and Computer Science
    An extensive collection of links, including images, articles and reports, documenting the history of women and computers. (Added February 15, 1999.)
    Women in Technology International
    "Women in Technology International, founded in 1989, is a rapidly growing association of more than 6,000 members, 95% of whom are professional women working in technology organizations. WITI is dedicated to: increasing the number of women in executive roles, helping women become more financially independent and technology-literate [and] encouraging young women to choose careers in science and technology." The site features information about events and activities, including an annual conference. (Added February 8, 1998.)
    Club Girl Tech
    Sports, games, electronic postcards, "Chick Chat," and more, all designed to enrich girls's experiences with technology. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    CMC Magazine: The Gendered Mystique
    The March 1, 1996, issue of CMC (Computer-Mediated Communication) Magazine was a special issue, "Women and Gender Online." Leslie Regan Shade reviewed a handful of recent books about women and technology. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)

  • Authors and Books

    • TPCN - Great Quotations (Quotes) To Inspire And Motivate You! - Women
    Funny, wise and significant musings. Use the site to find quotations about a variety of subjects and by a wide range of writers and thinkers. (Added February 15, 1999.)
    Free Your Mind Home Page
    Free Your Mind is a new book written by Ellen Bass and Kate Kaufman for gay, lesbian and bisexual youth and their allies. The site includes a detailed table of contents, profiles of the authors and links to other related sites. (Added October 19, 1997.)
    Feminist Bookstores Network Catalog
    "Over a hundred feminist bookstores across the United States and Canada have joined forces to bring you this beautiful collection of books, music and video that celebrate women's lives and work. Feminist bookstores showcase the most exciting books by, about and for women - books about our lives, our changes, and our challenges.... With your continued and dedicated support, feminist bookstores will not only survive the onslaught of the chains and super-chains that are trying to dominate book distribution in North America, we will thrive and continue to set the standard to which corporate bookstores aspire." (Added September 9, 1997.)
    Karen Kaigler-Walker, Ph.D.
    Dr. Walker is the author of Positive Aging: Every Woman's Quest for Wisdom and Beauty, wherein she "guides women through the myriad erroneous assumptions and preconceptions about the primal role of female beauty and aging." Please contact Sisterhood if you want to order a copy. (Added September 1, 1997.)
    Feminist Bookstores Index
    A directory of feminist bookstores and publishers worldwide, including links to e-mail and Web sites. (Added August 17, 1997.)
    The Orange Prize for Fiction
    "Despite its high media profile, the Orange prize for fiction was actually only launched in January 1996. It is the UK's largest annual literary award for a single book and celebrates the very best of women's writing from all over the world." (Added August 17, 1997.)
    Spinifex Press
    "Spinifex Press is an independent feminist press, publishing innovative and controversial fiction and non-fiction by Australian and international authors." (Added August 17, 1997.)
    Bookstores: Alternative Books
    "'The Alternative Books Consortium' is a collection of niche oriented booksellers that is larger than anything else of its kind online." Alternative Books is a division of L.A. Bridge, the Internet Service Provider that hosts Sisterhood's site. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    The Andrea Dworkin Web Site
    Nikki Craft has created a clearinghouse for information about Andrea, her writings and her ideas, free of the wrath and venom that so often derail discussions about this important, vitally active feminist. Nikki has added Dear Bill and Hillary , Andrea's response to the Clinton sex scandals. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)

  • Academic Resources

    • The Yale Journal of Law and Feminism
    Currently in its tenth year, YJLF is one of a handful of student edited law journals treating issues of women, feminism and the law. The Web site includes material from the latest issue, instructions for submitting articles and links to other law journal sites. (Added November 2, 1997.)
    Rosalind Krauss: A Selective Bibliography
    Krauss is an important art historian and critical theorist who specializes in 20th century Western art. In 1995 she delivered the Wellek Library Lectures for the Critical Theory Institute at the University of California, Irvine. The bibliography compiled by Eddie Yeghiayan of UCI is one of several he's compiled for the lecture series and is a helpful introduction to Krauss's work. (Added September 1, 1997.)
    The English Server
    "The English Server is a cooperative which has been publishing humanities texts online since 1990. Today it offers over eighteen thousand works, covering a wide range of interests." Included among the publications are texts dealing with feminism and gender and sexuality. The site also provides information about Carnegie Mellon University's Women's Center collective. (Added August 17, 1997.)
    Information Resources for Women's Studies, Women's Studies Program, U.C., Santa Barbara
    Feminism & Gender Studies, Lesbian Studies, Journals, Library Resources, Humanities: History-Literature- Philosophy- Religion, Law & Society, Other Resources, Publishers & Booksellers, Consortium. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
    Foyer WIF, formerly Women In French Homepage
    Conferences and other events announcements, job listings, scholarly writings and links to sites related to the study of the French language and literature with an emphasis on women's writings and representations. "This site has been created and is managed by Kathryn Murphy-Judy under the auspices of Virginia Commonwealth University BUT IT BELONGS TO ALL OF US." (Added c. August 3, 1997.)

  • The Internet and World Wide Web

    • Women.com Networks
    A site like Women.com belongs in a category all its own, but as a general Web resource designed for women, it's remarkable. "Founded in 1992, when 'women online' were still something of a novelty, Women.com Networks stepped in to become the first online media company to produce content and create community specifically for women." Sign up for a free e-mail account. (Added November 23, 1998.)
    Librarians' Index to the Internet
    Carole Leita's excellent index to the 'Net is not only for librarians. Ms. Leita collected and organized sites for several years as a librarian at the Berkeley Public Library. Her site has become so popular and extensive that it has migrated to a machine at U.C. Berkeley. She covers many facets of the Web, but particularly pertinent are her categories dealing with women and with gays and lesbians. (Added c. August 3, 1997.)
